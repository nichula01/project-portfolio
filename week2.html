Week 2 · Kelani River Dataset

Familiarizing with Google Earth Engine

This week I dove into the Google Earth Engine interface to collect RGB imagery for the Kelani River flood-prone corridor. The focus was on learning how to navigate the workspace, capture metadata, and archive 20 representative scenes for our baseline dataset.

Exploring the GEE Interface

I mapped the Kelani River flood-prone area inside GEE, toggled between Sentinel-2 composites, and annotated the layers that highlight vegetation and water coverage. This hands-on exploration helped me compile consistent RGB exports that carry the same scale and projection.

Geo-Documented Evidence

Every capture is tagged with longitude, latitude, and area descriptors so the dataset can be traced back to the original collection context. The reference document below summarizes the first 20 RGB tiles and their spatial anchors within the Kelani River basin.
