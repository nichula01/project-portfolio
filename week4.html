Getting Hands-On with Annotation and Research

Week 4 centered on mastering our group’s annotation portal, documenting each screen capture, and digesting remote sensing datasets plus unsupervised change detection papers.

Dataset Annotation Interface

Remote Sensing Dataset Papers

I reviewed the two published dataset papers that live in the `papers/` folder. These readings provided insights into the sensor setups, label schemes, and evaluation splits that frame our annotations.

  • WHU-CD dataset paper – Download whucd.pdf to revisit the tile statistics, building change metrics, and the open benchmarks that inspired our workflow. The same article is available through the MDPI Remote Sensing website at https://doi.org/10.3390/rs17020178.
  • Second dataset paper – Download second.pdf for details on the sensor fusion experiments and the publicly released masks referenced in our weekly notes. Its publisher page on IEEE Xplore is at https://doi.org/10.1109/TGRS.2024.3425815.

Studying Unsupervised Change Detection

Inspired by the Remote Sens. 2024 paper An Unsupervised Remote Sensing Image Change Detection Method Based on RVMamba and Posterior Probability Space Change Vector, I followed the same “Open Access Article” structure (title, authors, DOI, submission dates) to keep the metadata intact. The paper introduces the RFCC framework built on UCVAPS and outlines how RVMamba and CSBN remove the need for heavy user tuning.

  • UCVAPS extends CVAPS to avoid radiometric correction and classification drift by normalizing change magnitudes before thresholding.
  • RVMamba feature clustering provides high-quality segments that feed the CSBN module with training samples for the posterior probability estimation.
  • Fuzzy C-means decomposes mixed pixels, while the CSBN brings spatial context so the final change-magnitude map works well on HRRS imagery.

The paper is stored at papers/unsupervised.pdf and demonstrates how a combination of unsupervised segmentation and Bayesian inference can outperform supervised baselines on seven datasets.
